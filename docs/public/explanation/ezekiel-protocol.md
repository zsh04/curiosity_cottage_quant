## The 5 Pillars of Physics (Mathematical Constitution)

1. **Mass ($m$):** $m = V \times CLV$
    * Where $V$ is Volume and $CLV$ = $\frac{(C - L) - (H - C)}{H - L}$.
    * *Rationale:* Measures the "Push" of the candle relative to its range.

2. **Momentum ($p$):** $p = m \times v$
    * Where $v$ (Velocity) is the rate of change of Price ($dP/dt$).

3. **Boyd Friction ($\gamma$):** $\gamma = \frac{TradeCount}{Volume}$
    * *Rationale:* High friction means "churn" or hidden liquidity (The Swamp).

4. **Shannon Entropy ($H$):** $H(X) = -\sum P(x_i) \log_2 P(x_i)$
    * Calculated on the distribution of log-returns in the Ring Buffer.
    * *Rationale:* Measures the "Chaos." If $H > 0.8$, the market is random (No Trade).

5. **Nash Equilibrium ($N$):** $N = \frac{P_{current} - P_{mode}}{\sigma}$
    * Distance (Z-score) from the "High Volume Node" (Mode) of the profile.
